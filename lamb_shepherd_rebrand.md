# Lamb Shepherd Restaurant — Rebrand Plan (replacing Buriram template)

> **Project brief:** Repurpose the existing Buriram MVP (`buriram-eight.vercel.app`) as a landing site for **Lamb Shepherd Restaurant**, a halal authentic Xinjiang/Chinese-Muslim cuisine restaurant in Desa Setapak (Wangsa Maju area). Same template structure (small-portion shareable Asian, bilingual brand mark, ceramic-blue accents). Cuisine swaps from Thai-Chinese fusion → authentic Xinjiang/Northern Chinese halal.
>
> **Why this swap works:** Both brands share core DNA — bilingual marketing (Chinese + English), small-portion Asian cuisine concept (Buriram's "small bowls" → Lamb Shepherd's "shareable Chinese-Muslim plates"), authentic regional identity (Buriram = Isan Thailand → Lamb Shepherd = Xinjiang/NW China), hidden-gem positioning. The cream + chili red + ceramic blue palette translates directly with minor warm-toning adjustments.

## 2. Design Direction (mapping from Buriram template)

### Color Palette (warmer, more rustic than Buriram)

| Role | Buriram Original | Lamb Shepherd Replacement | Hex |
|------|-----------------|---------------------------|-----|
| Background (primary) | Soft Cream | Warm Sand | `#F2EBDA` |
| Surface | Pure Cream | Pure Cream | `#FBF7EC` (keep) |
| Primary text | Deep Indigo | Charred Brown | `#2D1F18` |
| Secondary text | Dusty Slate | Earth Brown | `#6B5544` |
| **Accent (THE color)** | Thai Chili Red `#C7301B` | **Xinjiang Red** | `#A8261A` |
| Secondary accent | Lemongrass Green `#7A9A6E` | **Cumin Gold** | `#C9A24E` |
| Highlight | Lemon Gold | Saffron | `#E8B838` |
| Soft accent | Ceramic Blue `#3B5A8A` | **Silk Road Indigo** | `#2D4566` |
| Deep contrast | Charred Black `#15171F` | Coal | `#1A1612` |

**Why these:**
- **Xinjiang red** — slightly deeper, more rustic than Thai chili red; reflects northern Chinese aesthetic (vs. Southeast Asian)
- **Cumin gold** — replaces Buriram's lemongrass; cumin is THE spice of Xinjiang lamb cooking
- **Silk Road indigo** — slightly warmer ceramic blue, references Central Asian textiles + Chinese porcelain
- **Saffron** — golden marigold tone, spice trade heritage
- **Warm sand backgrounds** — slightly warmer than Buriram's cooler off-white, evokes desert + clay-pot cooking

### Typography (keep Buriram's choices, rebalance Chinese accent)

- **Display / Headings:** `Cormorant Garamond` (keep — same elegant editorial energy)
- **Body:** `Inter` (keep)
- **Chinese accent:** `Noto Serif SC` for "中国穆斯林餐厅" or short brand mark — used like Buriram's "泰国小小碗" approach
- **Numerals (prices):** `Inter` (keep)
- **Accent / handwritten:** `Caveat` (keep)

### Mood

Authentic-Xinjiang · Halal-Chinese-Heritage · Hidden-Gem-Upgraded · Small-Plate-Shareable · Bilingual · Silk-Road-Adjacent

Visual references: Xinjiang lamb-cooking imagery, hand-pulled noodle close-ups, Chinese Muslim cuisine photography, warm-toned ceramic plates, traditional Northern Chinese restaurant aesthetics. NOT: generic Chinese banquet, hipster Asian fusion, loud Cantonese aesthetics.

## 9. Strategic Note (compared to other 5 mockups)

Lamb Shepherd is your **most authentic-niche brand** — the halal Xinjiang positioning is genuinely rare in KL. This makes it:

✅ **Easier to differentiate** in a Google search ("halal Xinjiang KL" has very few competitors)
✅ **Stronger SEO opportunity** — they can dominate niche searches with proper content
✅ **Higher emotional resonance** — Chinese-Muslim diners often travel far to find authentic options

❌ **Harder to scale** — owner may be conservative about online marketing
❌ **Smaller addressable market** — niche cuisine, smaller customer base
❌ **Possible language barrier** — owner may prefer Mandarin business communication

If they say yes, this is one of the more meaningful builds in your portfolio because the cuisine itself is rare. If they say no, the design language still works as a portfolio piece showing you can handle bilingual heritage brands.
